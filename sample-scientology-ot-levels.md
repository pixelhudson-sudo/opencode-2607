**Quick Facts**
- **Founded:** 1952 by L. Ron Hubbard
- **Status:** Active (Church of Scientology International, Los Angeles)
- **Members:** Estimated 25,000–50,000 active worldwide
- **Core Doctrine:** Each person is an immortal thetan (spiritual being) trapped in a physical universe of his own making. Through a graduated series of procedures called auditing and training, the thetan can regain its native abilities — operating at full potential without a body.

## Origins & Founder

Lafayette Ronald Hubbard was a science fiction writer who published *Dianetics: The Modern Science of Mental Health* in 1950, which became a bestseller. He incorporated the Church of Scientology in 1953 in Camden, New Jersey, with a graduated system of spiritual advancement called the Bridge to Total Freedom. The lower half of the Bridge is auditing (counseling processes that address the "reactive mind"), and the upper half is the Operating Thetan levels — secret materials Hubbard wrote between 1963 and 1982, released progressively to members as they donated increasingly large sums. Each OT level was announced as containing the "final" truth about human existence, and each was superseded by a higher level.

## Core Beliefs

Scientology actually believes a person is not the body or the mind but a thetan — an immortal spiritual being with infinite capabilities it has forgotten. The physical universe operates on the "MEST" principle (Matter, Energy, Space, Time), which the thetan created but is now trapped in. The reactive mind stores "engrams" — moments of unconsciousness or pain that trigger involuntary behavior. The goal of Dianetics is to erase the reactive mind and achieve the state of Clear. The goal of Scientology is to operate as a thetan free of all mental and physical limitations — an Operating Thetan.

## The Bridge to Total Freedom — OT Levels I–VIII

### OT I — The New OT Level I

Released in 1964. OT I consists of a set of preparatory exercises designed to get the pre-OT comfortable with the process of "exteriorization" — operating as a thetan outside the body. The key exercise is "exteriorization with full perception": the pre-OT closes their eyes, projects themselves to a point behind their chair, and describes what they can perceive from that location. Hubbard wrote that a thetan can "be exterior and still be in control of the body" and that the body is merely a "mental image" the thetan carries around. The level is short — roughly 2,000 words.

### OT II — Freeloader's Drills

Released in 1964. OT II addresses "out-ethics" (unethical behavior) by running "freeloader's drills." The premise: if a thetan has taken unfair advantage of another being (a "freeloading" act), that act creates a mass that pins the thetan to the physical universe. The pre-OT locates a freeloading act, verbalizes it, and "locates" the body thetan — a parasitic thetan attached to their own. Hubbard wrote: "One can have engrams of a relatively modern type which lie between him and his extinction. The most modern of these is the condition of taking unfair advantage." The level introduced the concept that the pre-OT is never alone — they are surrounded by disembodied thetans that must be handled.

### OT III — The Wall of Fire

Released in 1968. OT III is the most famous level because it contains the creation myth. It is also called "The Wall of Fire" because Hubbard claimed anyone reading the material before they were spiritually ready would die. The level tells a specific story:

> "Seventy-five million years ago, a Galactic Confederacy existed. It was composed of 26 stars and 76 planets, including Earth, then called Teegeeack. The ruler of the Confederacy was Xenu (pronounced Zee-noo). He was a tyrant. Overpopulation was the problem. Xenu solved it by bringing billions of beings to Teegeeack, stacking them around volcanoes, and detonating hydrogen bombs. The thetans of these beings were ripped from their bodies by the blast and, caught in the explosion's wave, clung to the bodies of those who survived. These cluster thetans were then given an 'implant' — a set of false concepts — by Xenu using a three-dimensional motion picture at a location in the Arctic. The concepts included: God, the Devil, Jesus Christ, the Cross, and the entire structure of human religions." [OT III materials, published in Fishman Affidavit, 1993]

The level teaches that these "body thetans" are still in every human today, causing irrational behavior, illness, and spiritual blindness. The OT III process requires the pre-OT to "run out" the implant incident in a specific sequence, handling each body thetan by "acknowledging" it and persuading it to leave. Hubbard warned that "any deviation from the exact procedure will result in a psychotic break."

### OT IV — L10, L11, L12

Released between 1970 and 1973. OT IV consists of three "Low Steps" (L10, L11, L12) that address more specific body thetan issues. L10 handles "the degraded being" — a condition Hubbard described as a thetan so low it cannot be rehabilitated. L11 handles "unmock" patterns — areas where the thetan has created false realities. L12 handles "the games condition" — the idea that the pre-OT is trapped in someone else's game. The level is the beginning of "solo auditing" (self-administered processes) and is the first level where the member works without a professional auditor present.

### OT V — New OT V

Released in 1980. OT V requires the pre-OT to continue solo auditing on their own body thetans using a device called an "E-meter" (a skin galvanometer Hubbard invented). The process involves scanning the body for "body thetan clusters" and running them out one at a time. Hubbard wrote that a human body carries "millions of body thetans" and that the process of clearing them all takes "thousands of hours." The level has more than 200 pages of instructions covering specific handling procedures for different types of body thetan clusters — clusters formed by physical trauma, clusters formed by "implant" incidents, and clusters formed by shared group experiences.

### OT VI — Service Facsimiles

Released in 1980. OT VI addresses "service facsimiles" — a concept Hubbard defined as "a computation that a thetan makes which he uses to be right, to make others wrong, or to keep himself from being at cause." The level teaches that the pre-OT's own thetan created these service facsimiles as a defense mechanism, and that they must be "flattened" (fully run out). The member identifies the specific service facsimile they are using in daily life — typically a pattern like "I'm not good enough" or "I need to help others to be safe" — and runs it through a standard auditing procedure until it no longer produces a reaction on the E-meter. Hubbard called this "the most difficult level on the Bridge" because the pre-OT must be willing to confront their own least-pleasant character traits.

### OT VII — Clearing Body Thetans Completely

Released in 1980. OT VII is the longest, most repetitive level. The mandate: clear every body thetan attached to the pre-OT. The process requires the member to sit with an E-meter, list body thetan clusters from head to toe, and handle each one individually. Hubbard wrote that the process "goes on until all body thetans have been located, handled, and are gone." In practice, OT VII can take years. The level explicitly states that as body thetans are cleared, the OT's own abilities increase — telepathic ability ("intention communication"), the ability to influence others without speaking, and "space expansion" (perceiving beyond normal visual range). Members who complete OT VII are considered "Clear OT VII." Many report that new body thetans continue to appear, requiring ongoing maintenance.

### OT VIII — Operating Thetan 8

Released in 1982, only available on the Church's ship the *Freewinds* in international waters. OT VIII is advertised as "the final level, the apex of all OT technology." Hubbard wrote: "The state of OT VIII is spiritual freedom from the physical universe. The OT has full control over matter, energy, space, and time." The level includes the statement that "there is a hole in the universe at 7.8" — a specific spatial coordinate — and that the OT, by mastering this level, can "BE beyond the universe." The level claims that Jesus Christ was an OT VIII who "miscalculated" his mission. OT VIII also addresses the "Wall of Fire" more directly than OT III, claiming that Hubbard himself "pulled the Wall of Fire" (survived reading the materials) and that anyone who attempts the level without proper preparation will "die within two weeks." A 1993 court filing by the Church of Scientology confirmed OT VIII "is indeed contained in a box in safekeeping aboard a ship."

## Practices & Daily Life

Scientologists train on "the tech" through coursework at local orgs (churches). Daily practice includes "morning routine" (a set of procedures Hubbard wrote for starting the day at cause), "word clearing" (looking up any misunderstood word from Hubbard's texts using a Scientology dictionary), and "ethics conditions" (evaluating one's own behavior against Hubbard's Conditions of Existence scale). Members are expected to donate money for each course, each auditing session, and each OT level — total costs to reach OT VIII can exceed $400,000. The practice of "disconnection" requires members to cut contact with any person (including family) labeled a "Suppressive Person" — someone actively opposed to Scientology.

## Leadership Structure

The Church of Scientology is hierarchical. David Miscavige has been the Chairman of the Board of the Religious Technology Center (RTC) since 1987. RTC holds the trademarks and controls the release of OT levels. Below RTC is the Church of Scientology International (CSI), which governs local orgs and missions. The Sea Organization (Sea Org) is the religious order — members sign a billion-year contract and live communally in strict discipline. Only Sea Org members may handle the highest OT levels.

## Controversies & Criticisms

The OT III materials (the Xenu story) were entered into evidence in the 1993 case *Church of Scientology v. Fishman*, in which the court ruled that the OT levels were not trade secrets. This made the materials publicly available. Former members have testified that OT levels are used as a revenue device — each level is priced higher, and members are told they cannot progress without completing the previous level. The German government does not recognize Scientology as a religion and considers it a commercial enterprise exploiting members.

## Current Status

Scientology continues to operate globally with orgs in 165 countries. The *Freewinds* ship remains in service in the Caribbean for OT VIII delivery. The Church aggressively pursues copyright claims against anyone publishing OT level materials online.
