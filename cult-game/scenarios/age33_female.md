# The Veil of Distraction — Scenario

**Age:** 33
**Gender:** Female
**Duration:** 10 days

---

### Day 1

**Event:**
Your son asks: "Mom, why do you always look tired?" You laugh it off. But the question lands somewhere it should not. You are the default parent, the household manager, the emotional laborer. "Tired" is the least of it.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Tell your family you need more help — specifically | +4 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Say "I'm fine" and keep doing everything | -1 | -1 | 0 |
| C | Hire help once a week — even if it feels indulgent | +3 | +1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Martyrdom rewards sacrifice with silence. "A good mother does not complain." The angel does not need to appear with a flaming sword. The cultural expectation is enough.

---

### Day 2

**Event:**
Your husband reaches for you in bed. You are not in the mood. You have not been in the mood in months. He does not press. But the distance between you feels like a cavern. He rolls over. The space fills with unspoken things.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Talk about it honestly — what you need, what changed | +5 | -2 | +3 |
| B | Perform desire to keep the peace | -2 | 0 | 0 |
| C | Suggest a weekend away, just the two of you | +3 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Intimacy-Killer does not need a big fight. It needs exhaustion, routine, and the slow forgetting of how to touch. The body remembers what the mind suppresses.

---

### Day 3

**Event:**
Your mother falls. A broken hip. The hospital calls while you are in a meeting. You drive three hours to her city. Your father cannot manage the stairs anymore. The house is falling apart. You are the one who will have to fix this.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Research care options and share the load with siblings | +4 | -2 | +2 |
| B | Move your parents closer to you | +5 | -3 | +2 |
| C | Do what you can and accept you cannot fix everything | +2 | +1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Sandwich Generation has you between two mouths: your children and your parents. Both need you. Neither cares that you also need yourself. The pressure does not let up because you are the "strong one."

---

### Day 4

**Event:**
You catch your reflection in a window. You do not recognize the woman looking back. Your face is different. Your body is different. You used to be able to eat whatever you wanted. You used to be looked at. Now you are just... mom.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Buy clothes that fit your current body and feel good | +3 | 0 | +1 |
| B | Start a restrictive diet to "get your body back" | -1 | +2 | 0 |
| C | Write down what your body has done for you this week | +4 | -1 | +2 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Vanity does not age gracefully. It grips tighter as the target changes. It wants you to mourn the body that carried you through youth — as if a changing body is a dying one, not a living one.

---

### Day 5

**Event:**
Your best friend calls. Her husband left. "I thought we were fine. We were fine. He said he wasn't happy. How do you just stop being happy?" You hold the phone. You look at your own marriage. The question echoes in your own home.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Listen to her fully, then reaffirm your own relationship | +3 | -1 | +1 |
| B | Pull away — her pain is too close to home | -1 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Help her practically while protecting your own boundaries | +2 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Fear plants others' catastrophes in your garden. Her divorce is not your prophecy. But the seed of "what if" has been planted. It will grow if watered by comparison.

---

### Day 6

**Event:**
Your annual review. "You're a valuable team member. We see you as a senior contributor." Not director. Not VP. The same job you have done for four years. The man you trained is now your boss's "rising star."

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Update your resume and start interviewing | +5 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Accept it and keep the stability | -1 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Have a direct conversation about the path upward | +3 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Ceiling is invisible, soundless, and made of assumptions. It is not a conspiracy of one meeting — it is the accumulated gravity of a thousand moments when someone else was chosen.

---

### Day 7

**Event:**
Your child's teacher calls. "He is acting out. Hitting other kids. Is everything okay at home?" The implication lands like a slap. You work full-time. He is in aftercare until 6. Of course things are not okay at home. But whose fault is that?

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Talk to your child gently to understand the root | +5 | -2 | +2 |
| B | Punish the behavior to send a clear message | -2 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Reduce work hours even if it hurts the finances | +4 | -1 | +2 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Blame-Mother has a chorus of voices: teachers, relatives, your own inner critic. The question is always "what did you do wrong?" The question is never "what support do you need?"

---

### Day 8

**Event:**
You are folding laundry. Everyone is asleep. You realize you do not know what you like anymore. What music do you listen to? What book would you read? You used to have opinions. Now you have grocery lists.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Pick one hobby and reclaim an hour a week for it | +5 | -1 | +3 |
| B | This is just what adulthood is — accept it | -1 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Join a group of women with similar feelings | +4 | -1 | +2 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Dissolution erases you slowly. First your hobbies, then your friends, then your sense of self. One day you look in the mirror and see only roles: mother, wife, employee. The person you were is a ghost.

---

### Day 9

**Event:**
Your college friends have a group chat. It used to be active. Now it is quiet for weeks, then a photo of a kid, then silence. You miss them. You have not made a new friend in three years. Making friends at this age feels like dating.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Plan a weekend trip with your college friends | +4 | -2 | +2 |
| B | Accept that friendships fade and focus on family | -1 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Join a local group — book club, running, anything | +3 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Isolation is the final victory. A woman without a village is easier to manage. The exhaustion of maintaining friendships feels impossible, but the cost of losing them is higher than the bills you are paying.

---

### Day 10

**Event:**
Your daughter is 10. She asked you if you were happy. Not "are you happy with me" but "are you happy, mom." Her eyes were so earnest. You said yes too quickly. Now the question is sitting in the room with you at 2 AM.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Answer honestly when she asks again — show her truth | +5 | -2 | +3 |
| B | Avoid the question — protect her from your pain | -1 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Start making changes so your answer becomes yes | +4 | -2 | +2 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Legacy asks: "What are you modeling for her?" If she watches you sacrifice yourself on the altar of duty, she will learn that duty is the whole of womanhood. The lesson you teach is the lesson she will live.
