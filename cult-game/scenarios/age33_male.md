# The Veil of Distraction — Scenario

**Age:** 33
**Gender:** Male
**Duration:** 10 days

---

### Day 1

**Event:**
You are test-driving a car you cannot afford. The leather interior. The engine purr. The salesman says: "You work hard. You deserve it." Your wife would kill you. Your 401k would weep. But the engine sounds like freedom.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Walk away from the purchase and the fantasy | +3 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Buy it — you deserve something for yourself | -3 | +3 | 0 |
| C | Set a financial goal to buy it responsibly in two years | +2 | 0 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Midlife knows your exact price. It studies your regrets and packages them as purchases. The car is not transportation — it is a repudiation of every compromise you made.

---

### Day 2

**Event:**
You and your wife watch TV in silence. Two feet apart on the same couch. You cannot remember the last time you had a conversation that was not about logistics. You ask her how her day was. She says "Fine." That is all.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Turn off the TV and start a real conversation | +5 | -2 | +3 |
| B | Accept it — this is what marriage becomes | -1 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Suggest couples counseling | +4 | -1 | +2 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Familiarity kills slowly. It does not need overt conflict — it needs two people who have stopped seeing each other. The silence is not empty. It is full of unspoken things.

---

### Day 3

**Event:**
Your son is crying because he got a B. A B. Your wife wants to schedule more tutoring. The school suggests advanced classes. The other parents' kids are all in three extracurriculars. You are exhausted from just watching.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Tell your son you are proud of him — full stop | +4 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Sign him up for the tutoring | 0 | -1 | 0 |
| C | Talk to your wife about reducing the pressure | +3 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Achievement has its claws in the next generation. It uses your love for your child as leverage. "Push them harder, or they will fall behind." The behind is a fiction. The exhaustion is not.

---

### Day 4

**Event:**
Your father is losing his balance. The doctor says "mild cognitive decline." Your mother is drowning in care. She calls you three times a day. You live four hours away. The guilt is a physical weight on your chest.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Visit every weekend, whatever it costs | +5 | -3 | +2 |
| B | Hire a home caregiver and call daily | +3 | 0 | +1 |
| C | Send money and visit once a month | +1 | +1 | 0 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Duty offers no good options. Every choice comes with loss. The question is not "how do I avoid pain" but "what kind of person do I want to have been when this is over."

---

### Day 5

**Event:**
The doctor calls after your physical. "Your blood pressure is concerning. Cholesterol is high. We should talk about medication." You are 33. You eat what you want. You have not exercised in two years. You feel invincible. You are not.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Schedule a follow-up and change your diet | +4 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Ignore it and hope it was a mistake | -3 | +2 | 0 |
| C | Join a gym and meal prep this week | +3 | 0 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Denial whispers: "You have time. You will fix it later. Everyone has high cholesterol." The body does not care about consensus. It keeps score in millimeters of plaque.

---

### Day 6

**Event:**
A younger colleague gets the promotion you wanted. Your manager says: "He has fresh energy. You understand." You have been here eight years. Eight years of loyalty, late nights, and "we value you." The valuation just came in.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Ask for a clear path to promotion or start looking | +4 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Withdraw and do the bare minimum | -2 | +2 | 0 |
| C | Mentor the younger colleague with genuine grace | +3 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Resentment offers a poison that does not kill the target. It calcifies in your own chest. The company was never going to love you back. But the lesson is not to stop working — it is to stop expecting loyalty from a system.

---

### Day 7

**Event:**
An ex from ten years ago sends a friend request. You stare at the profile. She looks happy. She is living in a city you always wanted to move to. You click her photos. Your wife walks in. "Who's that?"

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Close the tab and tell your wife honestly | +4 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Accept the request and say nothing | -3 | +2 | 0 |
| C | Ignore the request and let it pass | +1 | 0 | 0 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Nostalgia curates a highlight reel of roads not taken. It erases the reasons you left. The fantasy of "what if" is the most dangerous story you can tell yourself.

---

### Day 8

**Event:**
You run the numbers. If you had invested the money you spent on dining out in the last decade, you would have $60,000. You have $3,000 in savings. The spreadsheet does not lie. The truth is a cold room.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Start automating savings today, no matter how small | +4 | -1 | +2 |
| B | Dwell on the regret and do nothing | -2 | +1 | 0 |
| C | Forgive yourself and build a realistic plan | +3 | 0 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Regret is a necromancer. It raises dead choices and parades them before you. The purpose is not to teach — it is to paralyze. The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best is now.

---

### Day 9

**Event:**
Your neighbor bought a new SUV. Your brother-in-law just got a boat. Your coworker is renovating his kitchen. Your kitchen is fine. But suddenly it does not feel fine. It feels like a statement about who you are not.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Practice gratitude for what you have | +3 | 0 | +2 |
| B | Start planning a renovation you cannot afford | -2 | +2 | 0 |
| C | Volunteer somewhere — perspective is a cure | +4 | -1 | +1 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Status is a ladder with no top. Every rung reveals a higher one. The boat is a payment. The kitchen is a loan. The competition is a game where the only winning move is not to play.

---

### Day 10

**Event:**
You wake up at 3:17 AM. The house is quiet. Your wife is breathing softly next to you. Your child is asleep down the hall. You have everything you thought you wanted. And you feel nothing. A void. The ceiling stares back.

**Options:**

| # | Choice | Virtue | Energy | Awareness |
|---|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| A | Write in a journal about what you truly value | +4 | -2 | +3 |
| B | Roll over and scroll your phone until you pass out | -2 | +2 | 0 |
| C | Wake your wife and tell her you are struggling | +5 | -2 | +3 |

**Demonic Note** *(reveals at Awareness ≥ 3)*:
> The demon Meaninglessness arrives not after failure but after achievement. It reveals that the destination was never the point. The point is what you become in the seeking. If you stopped seeking, the void is where you are.
