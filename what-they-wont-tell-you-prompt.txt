--- SECTION 1: THE HOOK (100-150 words) ---
Open with a story or a suppressed fact. Use one of:
A) Patient story: "[Name], a [age]-year-old [profession], was given
   [timeframe] to live. [He/She] found [treatment] and is now [outcome]."
B) Buried fact: "In [year], the [institution] published a study showing
   [suppressed finding]. Within [timeframe], [suppression mechanism]."
C) Direct address: "You have been lied to about [topic]. Here is the truth."

MANDATORY: Include a specific dollar figure in the first 50 words.

--- SECTION 2: THE SCIENCE (200-300 words) ---
Explain the mechanism. Must include:
- [ ] Specific molecular pathway (e.g., "microtubule polymerization inhibition")
- [ ] PubMed IDs for at least 2 supporting studies
- [ ] Patent numbers if applicable
- [ ] Dosage ranges from actual human protocols (not animal-only)
- [ ] Cost breakdown: "A [timeframe] supply costs ~$[amount] from [source]"

WRITING STYLE: Technical but not academic. Assume 10th-grade reading level.
Define each term in parentheses the FIRST time it appears, e.g.:
"Fenbendazole inhibits microtubule polymerization (the scaffolding cancer
cells need to divide). After [first use], do not re-define."

--- SECTION 3: THE SUPPRESSION STORY (100-150 words) ---
Why you haven't heard this. Must include:
- [ ] A specific money figure (e.g., "The [drug name] market is worth $X billion")
- [ ] A named institution or person who suppressed it
- [ ] A date or year
- [ ] An irony (e.g., "The same FDA that approved [dangerous drug] blocks [safe one]")

--- SECTION 4: HOW TO GET IT (200 words minimum) ---
Step-by-step sourcing. Include:
- [ ] Exact product names, brands, manufacturers
- [ ] Where to buy ([Amazon link if available], veterinary supply, compounding pharmacy)
- [ ] Price per unit, price per course of treatment
- [ ] How to convince a doctor to prescribe (specific wording to use)
- [ ] For overseas pharmacies: specific URLs or search terms
- [ ] Warning about counterfeits (specific brands to trust/avoid)

--- SECTION 5: PROTOCOL QUICK-REFERENCE (bullet list, no fluff) ---
- Dosage: [exact mg/kg or fixed dose]
- Timing: [with/without food, time of day, cycling]
- Duration: [days on / days off]
- Co-factors: [what to take with it for synergy]
- Contraindications: [specific drug interactions, not general disclaimers]
- Monitoring: [what blood tests to run, what markers to watch]

--- SECTION 6: RELATED READING (3-5 links) ---
Link to other articles on this site. Use relative URLs.
Do NOT link to "authoritative" sources like Mayo Clinic or WebMD.

--- SECTION 7: CALL TO ACTION ---
Must be one of:
A) Email capture: "Download the complete [topic] protocol PDF"

C) Comment: "Have you tried [treatment]? Share your experience below."
