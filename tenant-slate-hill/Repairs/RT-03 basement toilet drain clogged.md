# RT-03 — Basement toilet/drain filled with standing water

## Symptom
Basement toilet bowl is filled with standing water and won't drain; tub on the same line also backs up. Recurring sewage backup since tenant moved in. Root system issue: **deteriorated drain/septic pipes** per multiple septic companies — toilet and tub are on the **same pipe line**.

## ⚠ Important framing
This is **NOT just a clog** — it's a documented structural defect (T Webber/T Cooper said pipes are in poor condition). DIY clearing is a **temporary relief measure only**. The permanent fix is the pipe/line repair tracked separately ([[fix drain pipes]]). Do not let a temporary clear substitute for the real repair — that gap is the core legal exposure.

## DIY clearing — try in order (cheapest/safest first)
1. **Stop adding water.** Don't flush again — it'll overflow sewage. Shut the toilet supply valve.
2. **Plunger (flange plunger)** — proper bowl plunger, firm seals, 15–20 strong strokes. Already attempted repeatedly by tenant with limited success.
3. **Closet auger (toilet auger), ~$25** — the correct DIY tool for a toilet (not a flat sink snake). Feed the cable, crank through the trap. Clears clogs in the toilet trap/just past it.
4. **Drum/drain snake from the cleanout** — locate the basement **cleanout** (capped fitting on the line). Open it (expect water/sewage — have a bucket + shop vac ready) and run a snake toward the main. This reaches clogs farther down the shared line than the toilet auger.

### DIY water-pressure options (you asked)
- **Drain bladder / blow bag (~$10–15)** — rubber bladder on a garden hose; insert into the cleanout or drain, it expands to seal then blasts water pressure down the line. **Effective for soft/organic clogs in a sound pipe.** ⚠ Caution here: on **deteriorated pipes** (your case) high pressure risks worsening a cracked joint or pushing a leak — use low/moderate supply, and stop if you see water escaping anywhere it shouldn't.
- **Hot water + dish soap** (toilet only, never boiling on porcelain) — pour a gallon of hot (not boiling) water with dish soap from waist height; can free a soft clog. Mild, safe, worth a first try.
- **Wet/dry shop vac in reverse / suction** — set to liquid, seal over the drain, pull the clog back out. Already have the garage shop vac.
- **Avoid chemical drain cleaners** — tenant used Drano; ineffective here and damages aging pipes + harms the septic biology.

## If DIY fails
Call for a **mainline snake/hydro-jet + camera** from the cleanout. Given pipe condition, push the **trenchless/pipe-lining repair** already in motion rather than repeat clears.
