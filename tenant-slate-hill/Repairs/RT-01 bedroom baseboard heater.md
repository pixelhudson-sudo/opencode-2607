# RT-01 — Bedroom baseboard heater not working

## Symptom
Baseboard heater in the daughter's bedroom does not heat. Reported cold ("in the negatives") while rest of house heats fine. System is hot-water/oil baseboard (per house profile).

## Likely cause
**Thermostatic radiator valve (TRV) stuck closed** on that zone/loop. Single cold baseboard while the rest of the house is warm = no hot water reaching that loop, not a boiler problem. Common failure: the TRV pin seizes in the closed position.

## DIY fix (try in order)
1. **Free the stuck pin** — remove the TRV head (unscrew the plastic knob/cap). Underneath is a spring-loaded metal pin. Press it in and out a few times with pliers; a seized pin that won't move freely is the culprit. Tap gently, work it until it springs back on its own.
2. **Bleed the baseboard** — if pin moves but still cold, there may be air in the loop. Open the bleeder valve at the end of the baseboard run with a bleed key until water (not air) comes out.
3. **Confirm the supply valve is open** — check the zone valve / hand valve feeding that loop is fully open.
4. If pin is frozen solid → **replace the TRV** (cartridge or whole valve). ~$15–40 part.

## Parts / cost
- Thermostatic valve / TRV head: ~$15–40
- Bleed key: ~$5
- Pro visit if replacement needed: ~$150–250
