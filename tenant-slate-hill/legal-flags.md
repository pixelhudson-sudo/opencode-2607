## Flag 1 — Pre-Existing Damage Claim

**Her quote:** *"I took pictures of the water damage before I moved in"*
**Law triggered:** NY RPL §235-b (Warranty of Habitability)

### Risk to you
If she has timestamped photos from move-in (March 15, 2024), she can argue the basement plumbing was already defective when she signed. Under §235-b, landlords warrant habitability **from lease start** — pre-existing conditions are NOT a landlord defense; they are actually a landlord liability.

- [ ] Get written statement from E Cooper / any contractor who worked on the property **before** March 15, 2024 confirming condition at that time

## Flag 2 — Mold / Health Claim
**Date:** Sept 14, 2024
**Her quote:** *"it's posing a health issue with all the water, mold and mildew"*
**Law triggered:** NY RPL §235-b (Habitability) + potential §223-b (Retaliation)

### Risk to you
Mold is treated as a serious habitability violation in NY. If she files an HP Action (Housing Court repair order), a judge can:
- Order you to remediate within 30 days
- Reduce rent retroactively
- Award her damages for discomfort/inconvenience

If she files a health department complaint **and** you then move to non-renew, she can claim retaliation under §223-b. The retaliation presumption lasts **1 year** from her complaint date.

### Remediation steps
- [ ] **Commission a licensed mold inspector immediately** — get a written report. If mold is found, remediate and get a clearance certificate. This protects you both ways: proves you acted in good faith, and establishes what's actually there vs. what she claims
- [ ] Keep all contractor invoices, remediation reports, and photos with dates
- [ ] Respond to her mold claim in writing (text or email): acknowledge receipt, confirm you are scheduling an inspection — creates a paper trail of good-faith response
- [ ] Do NOT ignore this claim even if you believe it's exaggerated

### Protection going forward
Your non-renewal decision is dated **before** any formal inspector complaint was filed. Document that your intent to not renew predates any complaint (use this conversation log + any notes you made). This is your retaliation defense.

## Flag 5 — Lease Renewal + Rent Reduction Demand + Inspector Threat
**Date:** April 1, 2026 (today — lease expiration day)
**Her quote:** *"I may need to involve a licensed inspector due to health and safety concerns"* + *"I would like to discuss a temporary rent reduction until the situation is fully rectified"*
**Law triggered:** NY RPL §223-b (Retaliation) + Good Cause Eviction Law (April 2024)

### Risk to you
This is the highest-risk flag. She has done three things in one message:
1. Asked to renew the lease
2. Demanded a rent reduction
3. Threatened to involve a health inspector

Under NY's **Good Cause Eviction Law** (enacted April 20, 2024), free-market landlords in many NY municipalities may now be required to offer lease renewals at a "reasonable rent increase." Check whether your municipality has opted IN to Good Cause (many upstate towns have not — this is critical to verify).

If you refuse renewal AND she then files an inspector complaint, she will argue retaliation. However, **your retaliation defense is strong** because:
- Your decision to not renew predates her complaint
- You have an 18-month documented repair effort showing good faith
- Her lease expired naturally — you are not terminating mid-lease

### Remediation steps
- [ ] **Verify immediately: Is your municipality covered by Good Cause Eviction Law?** Many upstate/Orange County towns opted out. If not covered, you have full non-renewal rights with proper notice
- [ ] **Serve a 30-Day Notice of Non-Renewal TODAY via certified mail + in-person delivery.** This establishes your non-renewal predates any complaint she files
- [ ] Do NOT respond to her rent reduction request in writing without legal advice — any concession in writing could be used against you
- [ ] Do NOT reference her inspector threat in your response — treat it as if it was not said. Your reply should be purely about the lease status
- [ ] Consult a local landlord-tenant attorney (Orange County / Hudson Valley area) before responding formally
