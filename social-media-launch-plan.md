# Social Launch Plan — "The Landlord Strikes Back" ($19)

## 1. Positioning

**One-liner:** *"Tenants have TikTok lawyers, free attorneys, and AI drafting their court filings. You have 14 days to return a deposit. This playbook is how you stop losing."*

**The enemy is information asymmetry — not tenants.** Never market hostility toward tenants; market *professionalization*. This keeps you on the right side of platform policies, fair-housing optics, and reality: the product's actual thesis is "out-comply them."

**Brand voice:** battle-scarred small landlord, not guru. "I got burned by a tenant who knew the law better than I did. Then I built the system." Your real story ("it happened to me") is the entire top of funnel.

**Audience (in priority order):**
1. Small NYC landlords (2–20 units) — highest pain, most exposed post-HSTPA
2. Accidental/inherited landlords (family brownstone, moved but kept the unit)
3. Tri-state small-portfolio owners
4. Secondary: national landlords (frame NY as "the hardest mode — if you can operate here, anywhere is easy")

- **$19 price psychology:** impulse tier, no-brainer vs. one hour of a lawyer ($400+). Anchor on-page against "one lost abatement claim = $1,500+."
- **Order bump to $29:** template pack in editable format. Expect 25–40% take rate.
- **Honest urgency only:** launch-week pricing that actually changes ($19 → $29 after day 7), or a real live Q&A for launch buyers. Never fake countdown timers or fake "only 3 left" — that's FTC bait and kills trust.
- **30-day refund guarantee** — increases conversions more than it costs; put it on the page.

## 3. Sales Page Skeleton

1. **Hook headline:** "My tenant knew NY law better than my lawyer. It cost me $4,000. Never again."
2. **Story (200 words):** your real incident — the abatement, the DHCR letter, the lesson. Specifics sell.
3. **The shift:** bullets on HSTPA, Right to Counsel, JustFix/AI tools, Good Cause — "the game changed, nobody told landlords."
4. **What's inside:** 12 parts + 10 templates, listed with one-line benefits (not features).
5. **Who it's for / not for** (for: small NY landlords; not for: anyone looking to cut corners — this is a lawful-compliance system).
6. **Guarantee + price + CTA.**
7. **Disclaimer block** (educational purposes only, not legal advice) — visible, not buried.

## 4. Content Engine

### Pillars (rotate daily)
1. **"It happened to me" story-time** — your incidents, mistakes, what the tenant did right. Highest-converting pillar.
2. **60-second statute bombs** — one law, one consequence, one fix.
3. **Myth-busting** — "You CAN'T just change the locks" / "You CAN'T keep the deposit for repainting."
4. **Template teardowns** — show the 5-day notice, explain why it wins cases.
5. **News reactions** — RGB rent votes, Good Cause updates, Housing Court changes. Ride algorithm waves.

### 10 Hooks That Stop the Scroll
1. "This 14-day deadline cost a Queens landlord six grand — and it's in YOUR lease right now."
2. "Your tenant can sue you for TRIPLE damages. Here's the one filing that triggers it."
3. "I lost $4,000 to a tenant with a spreadsheet. Everything she did was legal."
4. "If your tenant pays cash and you skip this receipt, you just lost your eviction case."
5. "NYC gives tenants free lawyers. Here's the 5-day certified letter that beats them."
6. "JustFix will build your tenant's lawsuit for free. This is the defense."
7. "The deposit mistake 90% of small landlords make in the first 5 seconds of a dispute."
8. "'Rent withholding' is only scary if your repair log looks like THIS."
9. "3 words you should never text a tenant. (Harassment law is real.)"
10. "My tenant's AI wrote a better demand letter than my attorney. So I wrote this."

### Rules for every post
- Real statutes, real numbers, verifiable — fact-check every claim (your audience WILL check, and landlords' groups police their own).
- CTA rotates: 70% "free audit in bio," 20% direct to $19 page, 10% follow-only.
- One idea per video. 25–45 seconds. Caption with the statute cited.

## 6. Platform Notes

- **TikTok / Reels / Shorts:** same vertical video, native captions, post all three. Consistency > polish.
- **YouTube:** weekly 8–12 min deep dives ("How a nonpayment case actually works in NYC Housing Court") — these rank in search and compound.
- **Facebook Groups:** where small landlords actually live. Answer questions for 2 weeks *before* ever mentioning the product; when you do, it's the free audit, not a sales link.
- **X:** policy/news reactions, screenshot threads of statutes, connect with RE investor accounts.
- **Reddit (r/Landlord, r/NYCapartments carefully):** value-first answers; profile link only. Never drop sales links — bans and brand damage.
- **LinkedIn:** for the small-portfolio/professional segment — the "professionalize or perish" angle plays well here.

## 7. Email Sequence (5 days)

1. **Delivery + story:** here's your audit; here's what happened to me.
2. **The 3 most expensive mistakes** (deposit timing, 5-day notice, repair silence).
3. **Teach:** how a habitability abatement is actually calculated — and how the response log kills it.
4. **Offer:** the playbook, what's inside, guarantee.
5. **Last call + FAQ** (real objections: "I'm not in NYC" → 70% is statewide law; "I only have 1 unit" → you have the most to lose).

## 8. Stay Clean (Legal & Platform Guardrails)

- **Educational disclaimer** on the product, sales page, and pinned in social bios: "Educational content, not legal advice."
- **No fabricated testimonials or results.** FTC enforces; screenshots are forever. Use real beta buyers — give 10 copies to landlord-group members for honest reviews.
- **No fake scarcity.** Only urgency that's true.
- **Never advise anything unlawful in content** (no lockout "tricks," no deposit "loopholes," no anti-voucher framing). Besides the liability, it's the fastest path to platform strikes and a torched brand. The lawful-compliance angle *is* the brand.
- **Don't claim outcomes** ("never lose a case") — claim process ("never hand them a case").
- **AI-content and ad policies:** if you run paid ads later, housing-adjacent ads have special category rules on Meta — organic-first avoids this entirely at launch.

## 9. Metrics That Matter (check weekly)

- Hook retention (3-second hold) on shorts — target 70%+; rewrite hooks, not whole videos
- Profile → link CTR — target 3%+
- Free-audit email capture — target 25%+ of link clicks
- Email → sale conversion — 1–3% is healthy at $19 cold traffic; 5%+ from the story-engaged list
- Order-bump take rate — target 25%+

Double down on whichever pillar produces email captures; kill what doesn't after 2 weeks, not 2 days.
