# Play 8: AI-Powered Dispute Letter Service

## What It Is

A service that generates professional dispute and demand letters for local businesses trying to collect money they're owed. The business owner answers 5 questions, AI drafts a formatted letter on letterhead, they review and send.

### Specific Verticals & Letter Types

| Vertical | Letter Type | Typical Amount | Success Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auto body shops | Demand letter for unpaid repairs | $500-$5,000 | ~60% pay within 14 days |
| Contractors | Final payment demand + lien warning | $2,000-$50,000 | ~50% pay after letter |
| Landlords/PMs | Security deposit dispute | $500-$5,000 | ~40% pay after letter |
| Freelancers/subcontractors | Non-payment / late payment | $500-$20,000 | ~60% pay within 21 days |
| Medical/dental practices | Patient balance collection | $100-$2,000 | ~55% pay within 30 days |
| Restaurants/catering | Event balance collection | $500-$10,000 | ~45% pay after letter |

## Real Example: Auto Body Shop Demand Letter

**Situation:** Customer picked up car, said they'd "send a check," never did. 47 days past due for $2,340. Shop owner has sent 3 text messages, 2 voicemails, no response.

**Input form (the owner fills this in 3 minutes):**
```
Your business: Austin Auto Body
Client name: Derek Mobley
Amount owed: $2,340.00
Date of service: June 3, 2026
Invoice number: INV-2026-4412
Payment due date: June 10, 2026
Communication attempts: 3 texts, 2 calls, 1 email (Jun 11, Jun 20, Jul 1, Jul 8)
Any agreements: Customer stated "I'll send a check this week" on June 10
```

**AI-generated letter:**

> **AUSTIN AUTO BODY**
> 2901 S Congress Ave, Austin, TX 78704
> (512) 555-0182  |  austinautobody.com
>
> July 17, 2026
>
> **VIA CERTIFIED MAIL — RETURN RECEIPT REQUESTED**
>
> Derek Mobley
> 4823 Red River St
> Austin, TX 78751
>
> **Re: FINAL NOTICE — Past Due Balance of $2,340.00**
>
> Mr. Mobley,
>
> This letter serves as formal notice that your account with Austin Auto Body remains unpaid in the amount of **$2,340.00** for services rendered on June 3, 2026 (Invoice #INV-2026-4412).
>
> Despite our attempts to contact you by text (June 11, June 20, July 1), phone (June 11, June 20), and email (July 8), we have received no payment and no response.
>
> **Payment is due within 10 calendar days** from the date of this letter. You may remit payment by:
>
>   • Credit card: pay.austinautobody.com
>   • Check: 2901 S Congress Ave, Austin, TX 78704
>   • Phone: (512) 555-0182
>
> If payment is not received by July 27, 2026, Austin Auto Body will pursue all available remedies, which may include:
>
>   1. Filing a claim in Travis County Small Claims Court (up to $20,000 jurisdiction)
>   2. Reporting this debt to credit reporting agencies
>   3. Assessment of interest at 1.5% per month as allowed by Texas law
>
> We would prefer to resolve this amicably without court involvement. Please contact us immediately to arrange payment.
>
> Sincerely,
>
> Marcus Webb
> Owner, Austin Auto Body

**Why this works:** It's official-looking (certified mail header, numbered remedies), it establishes a clear deadline, it creates a paper trail, and it escalates in tone from friendly texts to formal demand. The recipient knows this isn't just another text they can ignore.

## Real Example: Contractor Non-Payment (with Lien Warning)

**Situation:** Landscaper completed $8,400 patio job. Homeowner paid $4,200 deposit but hasn't paid final $4,200. 60 days past. Owner has called 4 times.

> **AUSTIN PREMIER LANDSCAPING**
> 1801 E 6th St, Austin, TX 78702
>
> July 17, 2026
>
> **Re: FINAL PAYMENT — Project #LP-2026-89 (Patio Install at 1203 Elm St)**
>
> Dear [Homeowner],
>
> This letter is formal notice that the final payment of **$4,200.00** for the patio installation completed on May 17, 2026, remains unpaid.
>
> Under Texas Property Code Chapter 53, Austin Premier Landscaping has the right to file a **Mechanics Lien** against your property at 1203 Elm St, Austin, TX 78702 for the unpaid amount. A filed lien will:
>
>   • Attach to the property title
>   • Appear on any title search for future sale or refinance
>   • Accrue legal interest
>
> We have no desire to take this step. We are proud of the work we did at your home and want to maintain a good relationship.
>
> Please remit the full $4,200 by July 27, 2026 to avoid lien filing.
>
> Sincerely,
> [Owner Name]

**Why this works:** The lien threat is real and specifically cites the Texas statute. Homeowners *will* pay to avoid a mechanics lien on their deed. Most contractors don't send this letter because they don't know how to write it or are afraid of seeming aggressive.

## Legal Boundary

These letters are drafted based on facts provided by the client. You are not:
- Evaluating whether the debt is legally enforceable
- Providing legal advice about collection options
- Representing the client in litigation
- Practicing law

The letter is a factual statement of amounts owed and a request for payment. Including statutory references (e.g., Texas Property Code mechanics lien) is information synthesis — not legal advice. The client decides whether to send, modify, or escalate.

The line you don't cross: writing letters that threaten legal action you know the client won't take (could be misrepresentation), writing letters that claim the debt is owed when the client hasn't provided evidence it exists.

**Risk: Low.**
